Place of Birth

Lena Taylor grew up in the era of Jim Crow and discrimination in the south. That motivated her to learn very quickly, study hard and follow in her motherâ€™s footsteps, becoming a teacher. Her life on the farm taught her values and skills that she still uses to this day. Having siblings helped her build people skills that allow her to work well with others personally and professionally. Graduating college from Jackson State University and later moving up north to pursue her career in teaching others. There she would find the love of her life and start a family of her own.

"Growing up I always wanted to help others and seeing my mother as a positive role model was very important. I saw the hard work she put in on the farm. Also, her educating others and being a teacher to other kids was something I knew I wanted to do."

Family Journey

She was 1 of 10 children born in a small town outside Jackson, Mississippi. Something she shared with her late husband. They were both from Mississippi and understood the effort it took to become successful early. They were married over 30 years and had two children in the journey of life together. She also became a grandmother as well. She would work for the school district of Wisconsin for over 25 years. She enjoys donating back to her alumni.
